# Risk Register Project

## Overview

This project involved the creation of a structured cybersecurity risk register for a fictional healthcare organization. The scope of work included defining a formal **risk acceptance matrix**, identifying and documenting **10 distinct security and operational risks**, and assigning **inherent and residual risk scores** based on impact and likelihood. Each risk entry included a clearly defined risk title, scenario description, ownership, treatment strategy, and justification.

The resulting risk register provides a complete, auditable view of the organization’s risk posture and demonstrates a repeatable approach to security risk management consistent with real-world healthcare GRC programs.

## Skills Learned

Through this project, I developed practical skills across the core functions of **information security risk management**. I gained experience identifying and articulating security risks in business-relevant language, including threats such as phishing, insider misuse, ransomware, third-party breaches, and legacy system exposure. Each risk was evaluated using inherent and residual impact and likelihood scoring, reinforcing my understanding of **qualitative risk analysis** techniques.

Using controls from established frameworks such as **NIST SP 800-53, ISO/IEC 27001,** and the **HIPAA Security Rule** I created risk responses, and evaluated their effectiveness through residual impact and probability. I also created business owner friendly justifications for risk responses and mitigations.

## Outcomes

The outcome of this project was a well-structured risk register supported by a defined risk acceptance matrix.

The project applied core GRC competencies including qualitative risk analysis, control mapping, and risk documentation, with a focus on translating technical security concerns into business-relevant risk statements. Risk scoring, ownership assignment, and treatment decisions were structured to support governance, compliance, and stakeholder decision-making within a healthcare regulatory context.
